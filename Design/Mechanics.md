# Mechanics

## Gears
### Description
The spur gear is positioned vertically and gravity-preloaded, held down by its own weight without an axle, meshing into two pinion gears below it.
Two pinion gears are positioned contralaterally, opposite to each other across the spur gear's sagittal<sup>[1](#footnote1)</sup> plane, providing opposing tangential and radial reaction forces.
Both pinion gears providing opposing reaction forces prevents radial or tangential translation of the spur gear's center.
Tapered retaining flanges on the pinion gears provides lateral restoring forces to oppose axial translation.
The driven pinion gear applies a tangential force on the teeth of the spur gear as well as a radial force from being positioned laterally from the spur gear's sagittal<sup>[1](#footnote1)</sup> plane.
Simultaneously, the idler pinion's contralateral opposing tangential and radial reaction forces combine with the driven pinion's, forming a force couple.
The combined forces of the two pinions apply a pure moment on the central spur gear, causing pure rotation about its geometric center.

### Analysis
The gravity-preload must exceed the radial separating force for the gears to retain their mesh without the spur gear lifting off.

$F_{r} = F_{t} \cdot \tan{\phi}$
